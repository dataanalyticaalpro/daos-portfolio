# DAOS – Day 4 Reflection

## Topic

Thinking Like an Analyst: Observations, Assumptions, and Hypotheses

---

## What I Learned

Today I learned that good analysts do not jump from observations to conclusions. Instead, they follow a structured investigation process.

I now understand the difference between:

- Observations (what happened)
- Assumptions (beliefs without enough evidence)
- Hypotheses (possible explanations that can be tested)
- Conclusions (statements supported by evidence)

One of the biggest lessons for me was realizing that assumptions are not necessarily wrong, but they should never be treated as facts. A professional analyst converts assumptions into testable hypotheses before making decisions.

---

## Business Thinking

Using the scenario of a 30% drop in online sales, I practiced generating multiple hypotheses instead of focusing on a single explanation.

Rather than asking, "What caused the problem?" I learned to ask:

- What evidence would confirm this?
- What evidence would reject this?
- Which department owns the required information?

This exercise reinforced that data analysis starts with asking the right business questions before looking at data.

---

## What Changed in My Thinking

Before today, I naturally wanted to explain problems immediately.

Today I learned to separate what I know from what I believe.

Instead of defending opinions, I now think in terms of investigations backed by evidence.

---

## Evidence of Learning

Today I can independently:

- Distinguish observations from assumptions and hypotheses.
- Generate multiple testable hypotheses for a business problem.
- Identify the evidence required to validate each hypothesis.
- Determine which business departments own the required information.

These are skills I did not consciously apply before this lesson.

---

## Key Takeaway

> Great analysts are not paid because they know the answers.
> They are trusted because they know how to discover the truth through evidence.
