# Day 3 – Understanding the Business Before the Data

## DAOS
**Month 1 • Week 1 • Day 3**

---

# Business Scenario

Maya Chen, Head of Analytics at NovaMart, informs me that sales dropped by **18% yesterday**.

Before analyzing any data, my responsibility is to understand the business problem rather than immediately opening Excel or SQL.

---

# Objective

Understand the business process behind the sales decline and generate hypotheses that can later be tested with data.

---

# My Understanding

A business problem happens before data exists.

Data does not create business problems; it provides evidence of what happened.

Before writing queries or analyzing reports, I must understand the business process and identify the right questions to ask.

---

# Questions I Would Ask

- What changed?
- Compared to what?
- When did it change?
- Where did it happen?
- Who was affected?
- Why might this have happened?
- How can I prove it?

---

# Hypotheses

## 1. Inventory shortage

**Hypothesis**

Sales dropped because popular products were out of stock.

**Evidence Needed**

- Current inventory levels
- Historical inventory records
- Best-selling products
- Stock-out reports

---

## 2. Low customer traffic

**Hypothesis**

Fewer customers visited the store.

**Evidence Needed**

- Daily customer count
- Historical customer traffic
- Store entry records

---

## 3. Poor customer service

**Hypothesis**

Customers left without purchasing because of poor service.

**Evidence Needed**

- Customer feedback
- Customer complaints
- Staff attendance
- Queue or waiting time data

---

## 4. Parking limitations

**Hypothesis**

Customers could not access the store because parking was unavailable.

**Evidence Needed**

- Parking occupancy
- Customer complaints
- Historical parking usage

---

## 5. Competitor promotion

**Hypothesis**

A competitor launched a promotion that attracted customers away.

**Evidence Needed**

- Competitor advertisements
- Marketing intelligence
- Customer feedback

---

## 6. Poor product mix

**Hypothesis**

The products available were not the products customers wanted.

**Evidence Needed**

- Best-selling products
- Product availability
- Product sales history

---

## 7. Decline in returning customers

**Hypothesis**

Regular customers visited less frequently.

**Evidence Needed**

- Customer purchase history
- Repeat customer reports

---

## 8. Bad weather

**Hypothesis**

Weather conditions reduced customer visits.

**Evidence Needed**

- Weather reports
- Historical sales during similar weather

---

## 9. Operational issues

**Hypothesis**

There were not enough sales representatives available to attend to customers.

**Evidence Needed**

- Staff attendance
- Shift schedules
- Customer waiting time

---

## 10. Price increase

**Hypothesis**

Customers bought fewer products because prices increased.

**Evidence Needed**

- Historical pricing
- Units sold before and after the increase
- Customer feedback

---

# Key Lesson

The role of an analyst is not to guess the cause of a business problem.

The role of an analyst is to generate reasonable hypotheses and use evidence to determine which explanation is correct.

Data is used to test hypotheses, not to replace analytical thinking.

---

# Reflection

Today I learned that understanding the business comes before understanding the data.

Instead of jumping into SQL immediately, I should first understand the business process, ask the right questions, generate multiple hypotheses, and then use data to confirm or reject each one.

This approach reduces assumptions and leads to better business decisions.
