# Day 11 – Symptoms vs Root Causes

## Key Learnings

- Businesses usually report **symptoms**, not the actual problem.
- A symptom is an observable outcome that indicates something is wrong.
- A cause is the underlying event or condition that produced the symptom.
- The analyst's job is to investigate the business process, not just analyze the data.
- Good analysis begins with understanding the business context before opening the dataset.
- The **Three Whys** framework helps uncover possible root causes by asking deeper business questions rather than accepting the first explanation.
- The purpose of the Three Whys is not to guess the root cause but to guide the investigation.
- Analysts eliminate possible causes by gathering evidence until the most likely explanation remains.
- SQL answers questions; business thinking determines which questions should be asked.

## Mental Model

Symptom → Investigation → Cause → Evidence → Insight → Recommendation

## Biggest Takeaway

> Analysts are not paid because they know the answers. They are paid because they know how to investigate until the answers become clear.
