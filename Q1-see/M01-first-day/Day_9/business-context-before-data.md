# DAOS – Day 9 Reflection

## What I Learned

Today I learned that data does not create business problems; it only records events and activities that occur within business processes. Before analyzing any dataset, I must first understand the business context, stakeholder objectives, and the success metrics being measured.

I also learned that different departments can arrive at different conclusions from the same dataset because they measure success differently.

## Key Takeaways

- Business comes before data.
- Every analysis starts with a business question.
- Revenue and profit measure different things.
- Marketing success is not determined by traffic alone.
- Different stakeholders require different insights from the same data.

## Evidence of Growth

I am beginning to think about the business before opening a dataset. Instead of asking "What does the data say?", I now ask "What business question am I trying to answer?"

## Independence Gained

I can now identify why understanding business context is necessary before analyzing data and explain why the same dataset can support different conclusions depending on the business question being asked.
